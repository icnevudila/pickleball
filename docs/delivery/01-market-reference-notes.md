## Sources

## What they do well

- `CourtReserve`
  - Strong breadth across bookings, payments, memberships, events, and public booking.
  - Proven traction in racquet clubs and multi-location operations.
  - Release notes indicate continued investment in waitlist and court sheet visibility.
- `OpenCourt`
  - Strong positioning around all-in-one operations, events, memberships, and payments.
  - More modern marketing language and community-oriented storytelling.
  - Good framing for multi-sport venues and player retention.
- `Chelsea Reservations Digital Signage`
  - Clear proof that digital signage is operationally valuable, not just decorative.
  - Real-time court sheet visibility reduces front desk interruption.
  - Announcement and ticker ideas translate well to TV liveboard behavior.

## Product takeaways for this repo

- Public booking must not be buried behind member-only assumptions.
- Waitlist state and payment state need first-class visibility in both admin and player surfaces.
- TV/liveboard should feel like a sports display, not a shrunk admin panel.
- Admin needs a compact control room for live queue + court operations, not a generic CRUD dashboard.
- Messaging hooks should attach to state transitions such as:
  - booking confirmed
  - payment failed
  - waitlist promoted
  - reminder before session

## Differentiation targets

- Better mobile-first booking UX than typical court software.
- Stronger external TV/liveboard with public-safe data shaping.
- Cleaner admin live control focused on court turnover and player confidence.
